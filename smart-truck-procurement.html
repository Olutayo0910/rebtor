Smart Truck Procurement in Nigeria 2025: Strategies for Cost-Efficient Fleet Expansion

In 2025, smart procurement is no longer optional—it’s essential for fleet managers and logistics investors in Nigeria. With rising demand, evolving import rules, and fresh financing options, every naira spent on truck acquisition must deliver value. In this high-stakes landscape, knowing the market, regulations, and funding channels isn’t just prudent—it’s your competitive edge.

Market Trends & Pricing Landscape

Nigeria’s Truck Market Momentum

Nigeria’s heavy-duty and freight trucking sectors are growing, driven by expanding industrial activity, e-commerce, and limited rail alternatives. These forces are fueling strong truck demand and investment opportunities. Sources: 6Wresearch, Trade.gov

Forecast reports indicate sustained growth through 2025 and beyond in truck sales, volumes, and market value. Sources: MarketScreener, IndexBox

Pricing Developments

Historically, average import price per truck spiked to about $48,000 in 2016, then moderated from 2017 to 2024. Source: IndexBox

In the local used-truck marketplace, listings start at ₦800,000, with a wide range up to several million naira depending on model and condition. Source: Jiji Nigeria

Import and Regulatory Considerations

Customs, Duties & Age Restrictions

Nigeria enforces a strict maximum 12-year age limit for imported used vehicles—only models from 2015 onward are cleared. Source: 234Drive

Automotive import tariffs remain steep—up to 70% combined duty & levy—though trucks and buses attract slightly lower rates. Source: National Economy

Smooth Import Processing

The Single Window Trade Portal streamlines documentation, customs clearance, and electronic payments from 12 government agencies. Source: Trade.gov

A 90-day amnesty window for regularizing certain vehicle import duties has recently been announced. Source: Vanguard News

Compliance must include up-to-date vehicle documents, strict adherence to age restrictions, and working with reputable clearing agents. Source: WheelMax

Financing Options for Truck Acquisition

Digital Financing Platforms

Autochek operates Africa’s first online truck-loans marketplace, offering access to vetted trucks and financing via partners like Kobo360. Sources: Business Insider Africa, Daily Trust

Moove, a Nigeria-founded fintech, enables revenue-based vehicle financing and plans to introduce 45,000 new vehicles by end-2025. Source: Reuters

Government-backed Credit Support

CREDICORP, Nigeria’s development finance body, launched “S.C.A.L.E.” in late 2024: a single-digit-rate credit scheme supporting locally manufactured vehicle purchases. Ecobank joined in early 2025 to distribute these funds. Source: Wikipedia

Traditional Loans & Leasing

Major banks like Stanbic IBTC offer vehicle and asset finance, suitable for both brand-new and used truck acquisitions. Source: stanbicibtcbank.com

Online platforms such as BuyCars.ng enable flexible installments for trucks and commercial vehicles. Source: BuyCars.NG

Brand-New vs Used Truck Procurement Strategies

Buying Brand-New Trucks

  • Advantages: Peace of mind with warranties, latest specs, and compliance with regulations.
  • Considerations: High upfront costs; leverage manufacturer financing or CREDICORP-backed loans when available.

Acquiring Used (Tokunbo) Trucks

  • Advantages: Lower capital requirement, faster entry.
  • Risks: Hidden maintenance costs, age limit restrictions (max 10 or 12 years), compliance risk.
  • Best Practices:
    • Use platforms with inspection reports (e.g., Autochek’s 150-point check)
    • Factor in maintenance history and potential repair costs before purchase.

Procurement Steps (Bullet Summary)

  • Define your fleet needs: capacity, fuel type, purpose (logistics, construction, etc.).
  • Choose source: new vs used; evaluate compliance, cost, and lifespan.
  • Secure financing: explore Moove, Autochek, CREDICORP, or bank loans.
  • Handle import documentation: CAC registration, Form M, SONCAP, PAAR via Single Window.
  • Evaluate duties and tariffs: consider high import duties, available amnesty windows, and age restrictions.
  • Select trusted dealer/clearing agent for transparency and efficiency.
  • Inspect thoroughly (for used trucks)—budget for repairs if needed.
  • Deploy and monitor performance, iterating strategies for efficiency.

Procurement in Nigeria’s current trucking landscape demands sharp strategy: understanding market trends, navigating import rules, and unlocking the right financing sources can dramatically improve fleet ROI. Whether you're scaling with new models or optimizing through reliable used units, smart planning wins the game.
